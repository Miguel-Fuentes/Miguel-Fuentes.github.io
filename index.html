Bio

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at UMass Amherst, expected to graduate in May 2026. My research combines probabilistic machine learning and differential privacy to develop methods that enhance data utility while preserving privacy across diverse applications. I have published at leading conferences including NeurIPS and AISTATS, and contributed open-source tools supporting privacy-preserving data analysis. Under the supervision of Prof. Daniel Sheldon, I aim to translate theoretical advances into practical, scalable solutions. I am seeking Research Scientist roles where I can apply my expertise to challenges in data privacy, generative modeling, and beyond. Open to relocation or remote opportunities.
